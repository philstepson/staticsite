In 1963, in my hometown of Memphis, TN, I was twelve years old when I passed both the Novice and Technician exams the same day and was licensed with my first call, WA4PSV. I moved to St. Louis, MO in 1976 and I upgraded to the General class and was assigned the call WBØVWY, then after moving to Atlanta, GA in 1984, I became N4LZR and after moving back to St. Louis.

I upgraded to the Amateur Extra license and changed my call to KØPWS to reclaim the Missouri geography and incorporate my initials, PWS. Currently, I'm a Enterprise Cloud Architect working for Oracle and have worked in the software/hardware technology industry for IBM, Amdahl, STK, EMC, Sequent and Ernst &Young. Technology and photography have always been hobbies but my degrees were a BS majoring in Economics and an MS majoring in Computer Science. (LinkedIn profile). 

I live in St. Louis City on the South Side and I'm in the process of installing new antennas while operating with a 40-10M EFHW dipole and Hustler 6-BTV vertical with 17meters. I'm using a little FlexRadio 6400 for HF SSB and digital modes, FT-8, JT-65 and PSK31. I'm also experimenting with DMR, D-STAR, C4FM - WIRES-X using handlhelds for DMR, DStar and Fusion with a Yaesu FTM100D, PC with SharkRF OpenSpot and Pi-Star. I've also seup a IC-9700, a G-5500 and EA4TX controller and will be adding 2M and 70CM XPOLs to use with MacDoppler software to work LEOs. 

Way back after I got my license in '63, my brother Mike followed and not to be outdone by his sons, our Dad got a license and became a consummate active Ham for life, achieving many DX awards and accolades. 

My brother Mike, (formerly WA4RPP and WAØZRV has now adopted Dad's old call WA4RQD so Dad's legend lives on. Mike lives close by in Arnold, MO. and I passed our Dad's 'ole TS-520SE hybrid to him, which has been refurbished, to get Mike back on the air and now he's updated his gear as well.  

When my Dad, Mike and I were all living together at home in Memphis, we used to share equipment and when we would check into the local 6M AM net, the net control would call 'The House of Hams' and the three of us would check in together - Great Memories!
